This website is dedicated to the early drum machnes and synthesizers that started hip hop and electronic dance music. Many of these machines were able to be found used or on the cheap. Thus allowing music pioneers a cheap way in to start experimenting and incoporating beats or rhythms into disco based sounds of the late 1970s.

The TR-909 by Roland
A part analog part sample based drum machine found standard in early house and techno music. Invented in 1984 to allow programming of drum tracks to accompany conventional music. Eventually, it found a home with electronic music producers.


The TR-808 by Roland
Released in 1980. The TR-808 was targeted for use to accompany studio musicians. The 808 eventually appealed to dance and hip hop artist because its bass drum which was able to produce extremely low-frequency sounds.


The TR-303 by Roland
Release in 1982. The TB-303 was originally used as a substitute bass player. It had an easy 16 step programming interface. It was instrumental in the beginnings of house music in Chicago and Detroit.
